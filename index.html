About Me

Hello! My name is John Albert. I grew up in the Pacific Northwest, and currently live in Renton, WA. When I am not writing code, I'm probably hiking, camping, road tripping, taking photos, or playing with my pup at the park.

Since 2021, I have worked as a Full-Stack Developer for Microsoft Viva Insights Core Services team through Launch Consulting Group. Some of the work I am most proud of includes Engineering Systems improvements around developer productivity, CI/CD Pipeline creation, deployment, and maintenance, .NET Telemetry improvements using Azure Data Explorer (Kusto) and building Power BI reports for product usage, engineering health, customer experience, etc.

I also enjoy projects in my personal Azure Subscription, creating full-stack web applications, an E-Commerce site for my family's business, and a personal portfolio.

Prior to 2020, I thrived in a busy, fast-paced work environment for five years, as a CNC Programmer and Shop Lead. It was then that the company I worked for closed its doors and I had the opportunity to pursue something I had always dreamed of doing, software development. The next work day after my company closed, I enrolled in Coding Dojo, an immersive, full-stack web development boot camp. I poured my heart and soul into the learning and am proud of what I can now accomplish. Building something creative from scratch is something I've found to be very satisfying!

JQZ Events

Technologies: C# - Asp.NET Core, HTML/CSS, Bootstrap, MySQL, jQuery, Microsoft Identity, Github, System.Net.Mail

Time to get that calendar in order! With the JQZ Events app, users have the ability to check their calendar, add events, and set reminders. Not only that, but they can also lookup friends using the app by email, send event invitations, RSVP to their others' friend request and invitations, and schedule free time.

I am very happy with the final result of this C# Asp.NET app. The major challenges came from building out a user friendly calendar, ensuring events wouldn't overlap, and coding a function to send out on time reminders via email. As it was a collaborative project, we relied heavily on Github for version control and code sharing as well as clear communication and teamwork to get the final product.

Collaborators: Quinn Lathe, Zack Springer, John Albert
